Introduction
The internationalization of financial markets has progressed hand-in-hand with advancements in technology, providing investors with opportunities to diversify risks and increase returns. It has also provided governments and corporations additional options to finance investments and fuel growth. With the growing inter-linkages between providers and recipients of capital across the globe, the ability for investors to assess the risks of domestic and foreign investments as well as the need for policy makers to regulate and ensure stability in their domestic financial markets, have acquired increased significance. 

Our Global Investor Friendliness Index (GIFI) is designed to measure the degree to which various countries are investor-friendly. With the publication of this index, our goal is to facilitate a greater understanding of global market structures and to promote financial market transparency. 

This beta-release of the GIFI provides the investor friendliness rankings for 143 countries across the globe, with the final release expected in Q4 of 2016.
